CRR Article 26 - Common Equity Tier 1 Capital

Article 26 of the Capital Requirements Regulation (CRR) defines the composition of Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) capital for EU institutions, which applies to UK banks through retained EU law.

CET1 Capital shall consist of the following elements:

1. Ordinary share capital (including share premium account):
   - Common shares issued by the institution
   - Share premium and related reserves
   - Must be fully paid up and non-redeemable
   - Must have no fixed maturity or redemption date
   - Must rank paripassu with ordinary shares

2. Retained earnings:
   - Accumulated after-tax profits not distributed to shareholders
   - Includes current year profits after appropriations
   - Must be available to absorb losses on a going concern basis
   - Reported in row 020 of COREP template C 01.00

3. Other disclosed reserves:
   - Legal reserve funds where required by law
   - Other reserves that meet CET1 criteria
   - Must be freely available to absorb losses

4. Additional Tier 1 instruments that meet specific criteria:
   - Instruments that are permanently written down upon trigger
   - Must be subordinated to all other claims
   - Must have no fixed maturity
   - Must be loss-absorbing on a going concern basis

Exclusions from CET1:
- Preference shares that are redeemable
- Shares with fixed dividend obligations
- Shares held by the institution itself (treasury shares)
- Instruments with embedded derivatives that materially affect their characteristics

COREP Template Mapping:
- Row 010: Ordinary share capital and share premium
- Row 020: Retained earnings
- Row 030: Other disclosed reserves
- Row 040: Other CET1 instruments
